Early Life and Education

Benazir Bhutto was born on 21 June 1953 in Karachi, Pakistan, to a politically influential family. Her father, Zulfikar Ali Bhutto, later became Prime Minister of Pakistan. She attended the Convent of Jesus and Mary in Karachi, Harvard University for Philosophy, Politics and Economics, and Oxford University for Law.

Political Career

Benazir Bhutto became the first woman Prime Minister of Pakistan and the first woman to head a Muslim-majority country. She served two terms: 1988–1990 and 1993–1996. She promoted democracy, women's rights, and modernization in Pakistan despite facing political challenges and opposition.

Key Achievements

  • Empowered women in politics and civil services.
  • Launched social welfare programs in healthcare and education.
  • Strengthened international relations with other countries.
  • Advocated for democracy and opposed military dictatorship.

Legacy and Assassination

Benazir Bhutto was assassinated on 27 December 2007 during a political rally in Rawalpindi. She is remembered as a trailblazing female leader and a symbol of courage and democracy in Pakistan. Her autobiography, "Daughter of the East," and her leadership continue to inspire generations.
